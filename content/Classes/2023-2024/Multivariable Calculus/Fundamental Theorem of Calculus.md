For function $f(x)$ differentiable on all points in interval $I=[a,b]$,
$$
f(b)-f(a)=\int_{a}^{b} f'(x)\, dx 
$$
In terms of differentials:
$$
df=f'(x)\, dx\implies \int_{\partial I} f=\int_{I}df
$$
